The questions it answers

What can a dashboard tell
a small business?

A business dashboard turns scattered records into answers you check like the weather. From the data most small businesses already have — billing, sales register, inventory, orders — a TheVincis dashboard typically answers:

  • What actually sells — best and worst performers by item, category, day and season, so the menu, shelf or catalogue earns its space.
  • When you're busy — peak hours and days from real transactions, so staffing follows demand instead of habit.
  • Where money leaks — dead stock, discount overuse, unbilled hours and slow-moving inventory, surfaced instead of buried.
  • Who comes back — repeat-customer share and order value trends, the earliest warning light any business has.
  • Whether today was good — one honest daily number against target, on your phone, without opening Excel.

The upgrade owners ask about

Excel reports vs a
live dashboard

Excel is more capable than it gets credit for — Power Query gives it live data connections too, and we're certified in it. So here is the honest comparison, round by round: Excel fairly takes the rounds it deserves; Power BI takes the ones that matter once a business runs on its numbers daily.

Excel reportsPower BI dashboard
Quick one-off analysisUnbeatable — open, pivot, answer in minutesHeavier than needed for a single question
Live data & refreshPossible with Power Query — refreshes when the file is openedScheduled cloud refresh — updated before you even look
Data size & sourcesSlows past a few lakh rows, one file at a timeLakhs to crores of rows, many sources in one governed model
Sharing & mobileA file to email or open in OneDriveA live app on any phone, with per-person access control
Daily effortYou assemble the answer each timeThe answer is waiting when you open it

Your Excel files don't get thrown away — they become a data source the dashboard reads automatically.

Before you ask

Data analytics questions,
answered straight.

Does a small business have enough data for analytics?

Yes — almost certainly. If you have a billing system, sales register, order history, inventory sheet or even consistent Excel files, you have enough data for a useful dashboard. Small-business analytics is not about big data; it is about answering specific questions from the records you already keep.

What tools do you use?

Power BI and Tableau for dashboards, with Python and SQL for data cleaning, modelling and automation, and Excel where it is genuinely the right tool. The founder holds Microsoft and Infosys certifications across this exact stack.

Can it connect to my billing or POS software?

Usually yes. Most Indian billing, POS and accounting tools can export data or expose an API; TheVincis audits your systems in the free consultation and confirms exactly what can be connected automatically and what needs a scheduled import before quoting.

How much does it cost?

It depends on how many data sources you have and how much cleaning they need. TheVincis starts with a free data audit, then gives a fixed quote for the dashboard build — sized for small-business budgets, with training included so you are not paying a consultant to read your own numbers forever.

Will my team be able to use it without a data analyst?

Yes — that is the point. Every build includes training for you and your team, from a founder who has delivered corporate data training to more than 200 professionals. Dashboards are designed to answer your daily questions at a glance, not to require an analyst on staff.
