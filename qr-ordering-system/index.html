How it works

How does QR code ordering work
in a restaurant?

QR code ordering replaces the printed menu and the order pad. Each table gets its own QR code; scanning it opens your live menu in the guest's phone browser, and confirmed orders appear in the kitchen in real time, tagged with the table number. Here is the full flow:

  1. Scan — the guest points their phone camera at the QR code on the table. The live menu opens in the browser; nothing to install.
  2. Browse — the menu shows photos, prices, veg/non-veg tags and today's specials, always up to date.
  3. Order — the guest builds a cart and confirms. The order lands on the kitchen screen instantly, tagged "Table 07".
  4. Serve — staff deliver food, not order-taking trips. Reorders happen from the same phone without flagging anyone down.
  5. Learn — every order feeds the owner dashboard: best-sellers, peak hours and table turnover, ready for tomorrow's decisions.

Before you ask

QR ordering questions,
answered straight.

Do customers need to download an app to use QR ordering?

No. TableServe runs entirely in the phone's web browser. A guest scans the QR code on the table with their camera, the live menu opens instantly, and they order — no app store, no download, no sign-up.

Does QR ordering work for small cafés and tier-2 city restaurants?

Yes — that is exactly who it is built for. The system needs no special hardware: printed QR codes on tables, any smartphone or tablet in the kitchen, and an internet connection. Plans are sized for tier-2 and tier-3 restaurant margins, not enterprise budgets.

Can I update my menu and prices myself?

Yes. The menu is live: you can change prices, add daily specials, hide out-of-stock items and update photos from the owner dashboard at any time. Changes appear on every table's menu instantly — no reprinting, no waiting.

What do I need to get started with TableServe?

Three things: your menu (a photo or file is enough), a device in the kitchen to receive orders (any smartphone or tablet works), and printed QR codes for your tables, which TheVincis provides. Most restaurants go live within days of the first call.
